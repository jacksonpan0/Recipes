Haemul Soondubu-Jjigae

Soondubu-jjigae is a Korean stew made with soft (uncurdled) tofu as a highlight ingredient. Haemul soondubu is a version that contains plenty of seafood.

Perhaps you're familiar with Sundubu or Soondubu Jjigae, but have you ever tried the Haemul Sunbudu Jjigae? Haemul refers to seafood in Korean, and this version is typically abundant with a seafood medley that includes squid, clams, shrimp, and even mussels! Along with the seafood, the stew includes vegetables and extra-soft tofu. If you're a seafood lover, you're sure to enjoy this delicious dish!

Ingredients

  • ½ cup chopped mushroom baby bella, can alter
  • 1 enoki mushroom handful, about 50g
  • ½ small onion diced
  • ½ small zucchini diced
  • ⅓ cup scallion chopped
  • ½ cup frozen seafood mix
  • 1½ cup water
  • 1 pack extra soft sofu
  • 1 egg
  • 1-2 tbsp sesame oil
  • ½ tsp salt optional, as needed

House Sauce

  • 1½ tbsp red pepper flake, coarse
  • 1½ tbsp Korean soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tsp anchovy sauce
  • 1½ tbsp garlic, minced
  • 1 pinch black pepper

Steps

  1. Soak frozen seafood in cold water until ice is melted, then drain well.
  2. Make the House Sauce, mix this sauce with the seafood and set aside.
  3. Wash and chop vegetables. Set aside.
  4. Begin by heating a medium-sized saucepan and adding sesame oil. Stir-fry the onion until it's partially cooked. Then, add the pre-seasoned seafood mixture and stir-fry for roughly two minutes. Next, add water to the pan and cover it with a lid. Once the water starts to boil, add in the zucchini, mushroom, and tofu. Allow the mixture to boil for about three to four minutes while the lid is still on. Finally, taste the dish and add salt if necessary.
  5. After adding the egg, give the mixture a good stir. Then, turn off the heat but leave the pan on the stove for one to two minutes, allowing the egg to cook through. Finally, sprinkle some scallions over the top and serve the dish hot.
